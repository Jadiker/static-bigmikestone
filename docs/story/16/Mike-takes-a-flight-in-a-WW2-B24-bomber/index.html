Mike takes a flight in a WW2 B-24 bomber

By: Tom Ericksen

when our friend Bill Schultz passed away, arrangements had been made to drop Bill's ashes from a restored B24 bomber. The morning we gathered at the airport to board the bomber, there were very high wind speeds, with swirling wind gusts. Naturally,  some of us were having second thoughts about going up in this World War 2 era aircraft. I pulled Mike aside from the others, I wanted his opinion about if he thought it would be safe to fly under the windy conditions. Without hesitation, he said, "Tom, just think of the headlines if this thing goes down. Local union members perish in vintage aircraft crash. We'll make the national news". We both had a good chuckle, Mike put my worries to rest. We boarded and up we went to drop Bill's ashes. Mike was strapped in over the open bomb bay doors.We were at about 12000 feet elevation. I can still here Mike's signature belly laugh as we flew over Forest Lake dropping Bill's ashes.
